Super computer in india

### 1. **PARAM Siddhi-AI**

- Location: **Centre for Development of Advanced Computing (C-DAC),
  Pune**
- One of the **fastest supercomputers in India**
- Built mainly for **Artificial Intelligence and machine learning**
- Performance: about **5.3 petaflops**

### 2. **Pratyush**

- Location: **Indian Institute of Tropical Meteorology, Pune**
- Used for **weather forecasting and climate research**
- Helps predict **monsoons and cyclones**

### 3. **Mihir**

- Location: **National Centre for Medium Range Weather Forecasting,
  Noida**
- Works with **Pratyush** for **weather prediction**
- Used for atmospheric and climate models

### 4. **PARAM Yuva II**

- Built by **Centre for Development of Advanced Computing**
- Located in Pune
- Used for **scientific and engineering simulations**

### 5. **PARAM Shivay**

- Installed at **Indian Institute of Technology (BHU)**
- Used for **research and academic computing**

### ⚡ Key Facts

- **Frontier** is the **first exascale supercomputer**, capable of more
  than **1 exaFLOPS** (1 quintillion calculations per second).

- **Fugaku** was the **world's fastest supercomputer from 2020--2022**.

- Supercomputers are used for:

  - Climate modeling 🌦️
  - AI training 🤖
  - Nuclear research ☢️
  - Space and physics simulations 🚀
  - Drug discovery 💊

💡 **Simple definition:**\
A **supercomputer** is a computer designed to perform **extremely fast
calculations using thousands or millions of processors working
together**.
